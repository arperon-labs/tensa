## How to sign

### Option A — Electronic signature via GitHub (recommended)

Contributions are accepted through pull requests. The CLA Assistant bot
will ask first-time contributors to indicate their acceptance of this
Agreement by commenting on the pull request:

> I have read the Contributor License Agreement and I hereby agree
> to its terms.

Your GitHub identity and the timestamp of your comment serve as your
signature. The bot maintains a signed-contributors list that can be
audited.

### Option B — Signed PDF (for entity contributors or enterprise legal)

If you are contributing on behalf of a company or your legal department
requires a signed document, download `CLA-entity.pdf` from
https://arperon.com/tensa/cla, complete the signature block, and email
the signed copy to **licensing@arperon.com**. We will countersign and
return a copy for your records.

### Option C — DCO fallback (minor contributions only)

For typo fixes, documentation, or other Contributions of fewer than
20 lines, Arperon accepts a Developer Certificate of Origin (DCO)
sign-off in lieu of this CLA:

    Signed-off-by: Your Name <your.email@example.com>

This is appended to the git commit message via `git commit -s`. Any
Contribution larger than 20 lines requires full CLA acceptance under
Option A or B.
